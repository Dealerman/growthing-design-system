> **Growthing** (growthing.info) is a full-service digital marketing consultancy. They run 360° strategies for their clients' economic growth using paid campaigns, SEO, web & app development, design, organic content, data/analytics, AI automation, and community management.
>
> Their tagline on the deck: **"Propuesta de Desarrollo Global del Ecosistema Digital"** (Global Digital Ecosystem Development Proposal).

## CONTENT FUNDAMENTALS

How Growthing writes — observed directly from the proposal deck.

### Language
- **Primary language is Spanish (Río-Plate/Argentine).** All decks, copy, and contracts default to Spanish. English is only ever a secondary translation. Use *"tú/usted"* sparingly — the deck is mostly impersonal third-person or first-person-plural (*"nosotros"*, *"nuestro equipo"*, *"enfocamos nuestras estrategias"*).
- **Formal-but-energetic voice.** Think consultancy, not agency. They sell to businesses, often traditional ones (cinemas, construction, public entities), so the tone is credible, technical, and methodical — never cheeky.

### Casing
- **Section titles: ALL CAPS.** *"NUESTROS SERVICIOS"*, *"SOBRE NOSOTROS"*, *"METODOLOGÍA DE TRABAJO"*, *"FLUJOGRAMA DE PROCESOS"*, *"PROCESO INTEGRAL DE AUDITORÍA DE ECOSISTEMA DIGITAL"*. This is a signature move — label eyebrows are always uppercased.
- **Body copy: sentence case.**
- **Proper nouns for tools/categories are capitalised.** *"Meta"*, *"Google Search"*, *"LinkedIn"*, *"Growth Marketing"*, *"Business Intelligence"*, *"Linkbuilding"*, *"CRM"*.

### Pronouns
- **"Nosotros" / "nuestro"** — the firm speaks as a collective. *"Nuestro equipo está conformado por…"*, *"Enfocamos nuestras estrategias…"*, *"Evaluaremos todo contenido…"*.
- **"El cliente" / "tu negocio"** — the reader is addressed obliquely; rarely direct "you".
- **Future simple for commitments.** *"Se firmará un NDA…"*, *"Se efectuarán diferentes Keyword research…"*, *"Habrá entrevistas individuales…"*. Feels formal and contractual.

### Vocabulary
- **Heavy jargon, used confidently.** *headcount, buyers, long tails, short tails, rich snippets, backend, UTMs, nurturing, scoring, dashboards, linkbuilding, off-page, on-page, CRM, NDA, KPIs*. They don't translate English marketing terms; they trust the reader to know them.
- **Bilingual naming pattern.** Spanish sentence structure with English technical nouns mixed in — very typical for Argentine agencies: *"Creación y optimización de contenido… Atributos ALT, Optimización de la velocidad de carga, Responsive design, Interlinkeado, Rich snippets"*.
- **Favourite phrasing:** *"Proceso integral de…"*, *"Análisis y auditoría de…"*, *"Estrategia de…"*, *"Diseño y comunicación visual"*. Three- and four-word formal noun phrases headline every section.

### Numbering / data
- **Two-digit step numbers.** *01, 02, … 09* in magenta, always with a zero-prefix. Used in flowcharts, pricing, and step lists.
- **Hours explicitly quantified.** *"25hs durante las primeras 3 semanas"*, *"168 hs de Front-End"*, *"508 hs de Back-End"*. Numbers carry authority.
- **Dates: `DD-MM-YY`.** *"FECHA: 16-10-25"*, *"VALIDEZ: HASTA EL 23-10-25"*.
- **Currency in US dollars with IVA line.** *"10 pagos de xxxxx + IVA"* — pesos are paid at *Dólar Blue* rate.

### Emoji and emphasis
- **No emoji.** The deck contains zero emoji. Do not add them. Emphasis is carried by weight (heavy/ultra Poppins) and colour (magenta), not iconographic flair.
- **No exclamation points.** Confident statements end with a period. Conviction comes from tone, not punctuation.

### Vibe — tl;dr
> Sober, technical, methodical. Spanish-first. Uppercased eyebrows, zero-prefixed step numbers, and specific quantities everywhere. The design compensates for the stiff copy with hot-pink energy.

**Example snippets** (verbatim from the deck):

> "Enfocamos nuestras estrategias 360° para el crecimiento económico de nuestros clientes basándonos en acciones publicitarias digitales y personalizadas mediante el uso de datos, inteligencia artificial, métodos predictivos y técnicas de Growth Marketing."

> "SOBRE NOSOTROS — Growthing es una empresa que se dedica a la transformación digital de entes públicos y privados."

## VISUAL FOUNDATIONS

### Palette
- **Hot magenta `#FF0084`** is the brand. It is loud, saturated, and essentially the whole brand signal. It's used for: the logo mark, step numbers, eyebrows, buttons, bullet dots, accent lines, gradient bars, wedge graphics, the round service-icon tiles, and — rarely — full-bleed panels.
- **Pure black `#000000`** is the workhorse. Headlines are black, not slate. Black is used at 100% — there's no soft-grey hierarchy in the deck.
- **Pure white `#FFFFFF`** is the default page. Almost everything sits on a white background.
- **Orange `#F1945B`** appears occasionally (e.g. slide 3 illustrative tints, slide 9 accents). It's a tertiary accent — not a second brand colour.
- No blues/teals/greens. No pastels except as the faded end of pink-to-white gradients.

### Type
- **Poppins, all weights**, is the only family. The deck embeds Poppins Ultra-Bold / Heavy / Bold / Semi-Bold / Medium / Regular.
- **Display:** Ultra (900) or Heavy (800), UPPERCASE, tight tracking, for hero titles and section starters.
- **Body:** Regular (400) or Medium (500), sentence case, `line-height: 1.45–1.7`.
- **Eyebrows/labels:** Semi-Bold (600) UPPERCASE with wide tracking (`0.16em`).
- **Numbers (01, 02…):** Heavy/Ultra in magenta, tight tracking.

> ✅ Poppins TTFs (Thin through Black, roman + italic, 18 files) are installed locally under `/fonts` and wired into `colors_and_type.css` via `@font-face`. No CDN dependency.

### Backgrounds
- **Default:** flat white. No textures, no noise, no gradients on content backgrounds.
- **Hero / section starter:** flat black with white Poppins-Ultra title, often with a magenta chevron / triangle graphic anchoring a corner.
- **Ornamental gradients:** thin horizontal bars (`#FFE6F2 → #FF0084 → #FFE6F2`) used as dividers, and large triangle/chevron wedges (pink → pale-pink, on a 135° axis) used as decorative corners.
- **No repeating patterns, no photographic textures behind content, no hand-drawn illustrations.**

### Imagery
- **Tonality: cool, corporate, slightly AI-generated.** The deck uses photo/render hybrids — a globe suspended over a boardroom, an executive standing amid network lines above a city skyline, a magnifying glass on a wooden desk. They all have a clean, over-produced stock-photography feel.
- **Warm vs cool:** mostly **cool** (blue-grey skylines, dusk lighting) with warm highlights. Never grainy, never black-and-white.
- **Subjects:** business people in suits, cityscapes at night, abstract network/data overlays, objects on clean desks. People are shown as anonymous silhouettes or mid-composition, never as portraits.
- **Never** use: candid snapshots, illustration styles, hand-drawn graphics, flat vector scenes with smiling characters.

### Animation
- The deck is static (PPTX). No motion language is documented. For web work, use **short, crisp fades and subtle up-from-8px translates** (`--dur-base` = 200ms, `--ease-out` = `cubic-bezier(.22,1,.36,1)`). No bounces, no elastic, no parallax. Keep it consultancy-appropriate.

### Hover states
- **Primary buttons (magenta fill):** hover darkens to `#E50077` (`--gw-magenta-600`). No lift.
- **Secondary buttons (black outline):** hover fills solid black.
- **Links:** text colour shifts from `#FF0084` → `#E50077`; underline stays.
- **Cards:** hover applies `box-shadow: var(--shadow-md)` and a `1px` magenta inner border. No scale/translate.

### Press states
- **Buttons:** shift to `magenta-600` and reduce opacity to `0.92`. Optional `transform: translateY(1px)` for tactile feel. No scale-down.

### Borders
- **1px solid** is the default weight — `--border-1` (`#D9D9D9`) on cards, `--border-strong` (`#000000`) for high-contrast chips and the pill buttons shown in the services grid.
- **Pink accent border** (`2px solid #FF0084`) used for active/selected states.
- No dashed borders except for drop zones.

### Shadows
- Used sparingly. Most cards are flat with a 1px border. When elevation is needed, use `--shadow-sm` or `--shadow-md`. The brand-tinted `--shadow-brand` is reserved for magenta CTAs in hero placements.

### Protection / capsules
- **Pill chips** for contact info and metadata: `border-radius: 999px`, 1px black border, uppercase label inside. See slide 15 (contact info).
- **Number pills** — the step numbers in the flowchart are inside soft circular bubbles on white, with a dotted connector arrow.
- No protection gradients behind text over imagery. When text sits on a photo, use a solid black band or a `rgba(0,0,0,.6)` overlay.

### Layout rules
- **Fixed elements:** bottom-right magenta triangle graphic on black section covers; a thin pink gradient bar as a top divider on content slides.
- **Grid:** 12-col on web. Deck uses ~1.5" margins on a 10"×7.5" slide; content lives in a central column with the decorative graphics bleeding to the edges.
- **Left-aligned text** is the default. Centred only for one-line hero titles.

### Transparency / blur
- **Rare.** The only transparency in the deck is the pink→white gradient ramps on ornamental triangles. Content is never set on blurred backgrounds. No frosted glass.

### Corner radii
- **Zero (`0`)** for section covers, buttons by default (the logo's square mark is sharp-edged), and hero images.
- **Pill (`999px`)** for chips, tags, step bubbles, and contact capsules.
- Nothing in between. Avoid 8–16px soft radii — it breaks the brand's hard-edge character.

### Cards
- **Shape:** hard rectangle OR fully pill. Never "Stripe card" soft-radius.
- **Default surface:** white, 1px `--border-1`, optional `--shadow-xs`.
- **Dark variant:** black surface, white text, magenta accent line 2px at top OR magenta eyebrow label inside.
- No glow, no gradient fills, no left-border-accent cards.

## ICONOGRAPHY

- **Primary icon style in the deck:** **round magenta tiles with white line-art icons inside** (services grid on slide 4). See `assets/services-icon-grid.jeg` — each service is a solid-magenta circle (~160px) with a white 1.5px-stroke line icon centred.
- **Stroke:** ~1.5–2px, consistent weight.
- **Fill style:** outlined, not filled (icons are line drawings, not glyphs).
- **Mini-icons in the flowchart** (slide 11) match — line icons inside neutral circles, usually on white with a magenta stroke.
- **UI-scale icons** (phone handset, globe, pins) on the contact slide are **very minimal line icons in black or magenta**, about 24–32px.
- **No emoji** anywhere.
- **No unicode-char icons** (e.g. ▶, ✓). They always use proper icon art.

**Icon library used in this system:** the deck uses custom/commissioned icons, not a library. For our web kit we substitute **[Lucide](https://lucide.dev)** (open-source line-icon set, identical stroke character). It's loaded from CDN in demo files:

```html
<script src="https://unpkg.com/lucide@latest/dist/umd/lucide.js"></script>
```

> ⚠️ **Substitution flag:** deck icons are bespoke. Lucide is the closest open-stroke match. For production work, obtain the original SVGs from Growthing's design team or re-commission the services grid.

### Caveats / asks for iteration

See the main conversation for the up-to-date list. Headline items:

1. **Icons are bespoke** in the source deck (round magenta tiles with white line art). The website UI kit currently draws inline SVG substitutes whose stroke weight matches the original family but individual glyphs differ. If Growthing can share the original icon SVGs / Illustrator file, drop them in `assets/icons/` and the kit will pick them up.
2. **Only one source deliverable was provided** (the proposal deck). There's no website scrape, no social-media templates, no existing product UI. The `ui_kits/website/` kit is constructed from the deck's visual language — a faithful extrapolation, not a pixel-accurate recreation. If the live www.growthing.info HTML/CSS or a Figma file is available, I can re-run and cross-reference.
3. **Stock imagery was not copied into the system** — the deck's hero photographs (globe over boardroom, executive on skyline, magnifier on desk) have unclear licensing. See `README.md · Imagery` for the art-direction brief so on-brand images can be sourced fresh.
